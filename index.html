Text-to-Image Generation

Core

Twenty-seven models that all claim to be "the latest." What actually differentiates them is which job working designers reach for them on — and which job they quietly avoid them on. Notes below pull from Reddit, Rival head-to-heads, ImageBattle, and hands-on reviewer testing.

Flux 2 Pro production default

The photoreal API favorite — the model designers run by default for everything that isn't a final hero shot. Loses ~75% of Rival's blind head-to-heads to Flux 2 Max but costs 20% less, so most production teams use Pro for iteration and switch to Max only for the keeper. Reviewer consensus: best skin, lighting, and material realism among hosted APIs.

Weak spotStarts misspelling small text once you ask for more than ~10 words on a poster; falls behind Imagen 4 on typography. Pick over Imagen 4 whenYou want grit and material realism over Google's polished, "safe" aesthetic.
Flux 2 Max hero shots

The "ships unedited to the client" tier. Wins 75% of Rival blind votes against Flux 2 Pro, with the gap widest on cinematic lighting, fabric/skin, and complex 4K backgrounds — the stuff that makes hero images feel like film stills, not renders. Costs 25% more per generation and takes longer; not for iteration.

Weak spotSlow and pricey enough that it's overkill for anything but finals; multi-character scenes still drift. Pick over Flux 2 Pro whenThis image ships unedited — portfolio piece, campaign keyart, fashion ad.
Flux 2 Flex

The under-discussed middle of the Flux 2 lineup — explicitly positioned for marketing teams running multi-asset campaigns rather than for hero generation. Accepts up to 8 reference images for character/product consistency across 50+ banner variants, which is the actual selling point. Most community attention skips Flex for Pro/Max above or Dev below.

Weak spotCharacter identity can drift across long sequences; multi-character scenes still fragile. Pick over Pro whenYou're producing 50 brand-consistent assets in one campaign, not one hero shot.
Flux Fast throwaway tier

The Schnell-family 1–4-step speed tier. Three to five seconds per concept at near-zero cost — designers and indie game devs use it to throw 20–50 wild variants at a wall before picking one to finish in Pro/Max. Apache-2.0 licensed and runs on modest hardware. One product designer's pattern: "50 logo concepts in under 5 seconds each."

Weak spotVisible quality drop on faces, fine textures, and any scene with more than a couple of subjects. Pick over Flux 2 Pro whenYou're exploring directions, not committing — the goal is volume and speed.
NVIDIA Sana laptop GPU

An NVIDIA Research model engineered around efficiency — 20× smaller than Flux-12B and 100× faster in throughput. Generates 1024×1024 in under a second on a 16GB laptop GPU; 4096×4096 in under 16 seconds. The Sana-Sprint variant pushes a 1024px image in 0.1s on an H100 via one/few-step distillation. Open-source under NVIDIA's research repo, fully self-hostable.

Weak spotText-to-image only — no editing, ControlNet, or LoRA ecosystem; quality ceiling below Flux 2 Pro on photoreal hero work. Pick over Flux Fast whenYou're self-hosting on a laptop or single-GPU box and need 4K output, not just speed.
Flux Pro 1.1 Ultra print-ready

The 4MP native specialist — generates 2048×2048 directly in ~10 seconds with no upscale step or artifacts. The only model in the Flux family that comfortably hands you a billboard-, magazine-, or large-format-poster-ready PNG straight out of the API. Raw Mode toggle strips the synthetic gloss for a more candid-photography aesthetic.

Weak spotPre-Flux-2 architecture: weaker prompt adherence, no multi-reference support, more text misspellings. Pick over Flux 2 Pro whenYou need native 4MP output for print and don't need Flux 2's multi-reference or hex-color controls.
Flux Pro 1.1

The original "Blueberry" model that put Flux on the map in late 2024 — still kept around for three real reasons in 2026: existing LoRA libraries that haven't been ported to Flux 2's 32B architecture, abstract/stylized work where Flux 2's compositional jump doesn't help, and budget-sensitive volume. Roughly 3x cheaper per image than Flux 2 Pro on most aggregators.

Weak spotFaces, embedded text, and complex compositions are all materially behind Flux 2. Pick over Flux 2 Pro whenYou have v1.x LoRAs to use, or you're doing non-photorealistic work where the upgrade buys nothing.
Flux Dev Redux

Not a base generator — it's Flux Dev with the Redux style-transfer module bolted on. Feed it a reference image and it pulls that art style into new generations. ComfyUI community workflows pair it with Depth ControlNet for portraits, or Canny ControlNet for room/architecture restyling. r/StableDiffusion sentiment when it shipped: "finally consistent style transfer after half a year of mediocre IPAdapters."

Weak spotSetup-heavy: ComfyUI, a separate CLIP-vision model, custom workflow files. Not a one-click tool. Pick over base Flux Dev whenYou want one image's look applied to a different subject — not just a similar prompt.
Mystic portraits ★

Reddit's pick for editorial portraiture and lookbook work — in head-to-head portrait tests, Mystic V2 "was rated the most realistic by far," beating Flux 1.1 (slight skin plasticity) and Midjourney V6.1 (overly artificial). Built by the Magnific team specifically for human realism and pairs natively with Magnific's skin enhancer for finals.

Weak spotAction poses, hands, and complex prop interactions — body morphing and missing fingers are recurring failure modes. Pick over Flux 2 Pro whenThe face is the subject, not the scene.
Higgsfield Image (Soul)

The "Soul" image model from the Higgsfield team (Snapchat AI alums) — built specifically for cinematic photoreal portraits, with Soul ID locking facial structure across a series. One Pollo AI reviewer rated it 9/10: "doesn't have that glossy plasticky look most AI generators have… genuinely looks like a real person." Sits inside Higgsfield's pipeline so you can push the image straight into Sora / Kling / Seedance for video without re-exporting.

Weak spotQuality is best on emotional close-ups and single-subject scenes; complex action and long sequences drift; $9/mo Basic plan only buys ~75 generations. Pick over Mystic whenThe portrait is the first frame of a video, not a standalone still.
Imagen 4 text in images

Google's text-in-image specialist. Hits ~95% accuracy on embedded type versus Flux 2's still-unreliable poster work — the model designers reach for when the image has to say something legible. Aesthetic is markedly more polished than Flux: cleaner light, less grit, less character. The default in Google Cloud / Vertex AI workflows.

Weak spotAnatomy errors in complex scenes (one reviewer got "a lady with three hands"); faces still in uncanny valley; less creative on abstract work than Imagen 3. Pick over Flux 2 Pro whenThe image has to render readable text — posters, ads, UI mockups, book covers, packaging.
Imagen 3

Worth keeping around even with Imagen 4 shipped. Scores higher on abstract/creative work in side-by-sides (8/10 vs Imagen 4's 6/10), uses a more forgiving short-prompt style, and runs ~50% cheaper. One reviewer flatly titled their writeup "Imagen 4: Not as Good as Imagen 3" for complex multi-subject scenes — the upgrade isn't universal.

Weak spotText rendering is the old ~70% — useless for posters; if text matters, jump to Imagen 4. Pick over Imagen 4 whenYou're doing abstract/illustrative work, batch generation, or writing short, loose prompts.
Imagen 3 Fast batch tier

Google's iteration model — four images in under four seconds at ~$0.02 each. The hybrid pattern most teams settle into: Imagen 3 Fast for prompt tuning and concept exploration, Imagen 3 or 4 for finals. At 50,000+ images/month the cost savings compound fast.

Weak spotVisible drop on lighting, shadow, bokeh, and fine texture versus full Imagen 3. Pick over Imagen 3 whenYou're iterating prompts, not finalizing — or running high-volume batch jobs.
ChatGPT Images 2.0 conversational

OpenAI's GPT Image 2. Strongest model for conversational generation and editing — drop 2–10 reference images in a single call, describe how they relate, iterate by chatting. Beats Flux on native multi-image composition. Known asymmetry: the ChatGPT web UI runs a hidden prompt-expander plus post-processing, so raw API output looks materially worse than what users post from the chat interface ("nerfed overnight" is the recurring complaint on the OpenAI forums).

Weak spotAspect ratios via API are limited (1:1, 3:2, 2:3); garbled embedded text despite OpenAI's marketing. Pick over Flux 2 Pro whenYou want natural-language editing chains or native multi-image composition in a single call.
GPT Image 1

OpenAI's original GPT-4o-based image model from March 2025 — the one that triggered the viral Studio Ghibli aesthetic wave. Lacks GPT Image 2's reasoning, web search, and multi-turn editing, but earns its place in production rotation as a stable baseline: more "neutral" output (won't auto-polish a rough sketch into something glossy), more predictable for bulk API workflows, and useful as an A/B against Image 2 before fully migrating.

Weak spotText rendering is the old broken kind — letters scramble on anything beyond a word or two; single-turn only, no conversation context. Pick over GPT Image 2 whenYou want the Image 1 aesthetic, need neutral non-polished output, or are running a stable bulk pipeline you don't want to migrate yet.
DALL-E 3 sunset path

On its way out — OpenAI deprecated DALL-E 3 from the ChatGPT UI in late 2025 and shut the API on May 12, 2026. Three live niches keep it in this guide: free generation via Microsoft Copilot (25/day, no upgrade needed), literal-prompt control for production pipelines that can't tolerate creative reinterpretation, and the same "neutral output" upside as GPT Image 1 — it won't auto-glossify a deliberately rough brief.

Weak spotMisspells embedded text ("Sumnner Sal 50% Of" is the canonical example); regenerates the whole image on edits instead of preserving the rest; quality clearly behind Flux 2 / Imagen 4. Pick over GPT Image 2 whenYou need literal prompt adherence without GPT's auto-polish, or you're using it free through Copilot.
Ideogram V3 typography ★

If the image needs readable type, this is the answer. One designer who ran 3,000+ images reports it "solved" the text-rendering problem that breaks every other model. The unlock is the separate "Text to include" field — put exact strings there, not in the prompt body, and accuracy jumps from ~70% across the field to the high 90s on short copy. The pick for posters, social cards, T-shirts, and YouTube thumbnails.

Weak spotFaces drift between generations; long body copy and multi-line layouts still fail; not photoreal. Pick over Flux 2 Pro / Imagen 4 whenText legibility is the binding constraint, not photorealism.
Reve V4 prompt adherence ★

Came out of nowhere in March 2025 and instantly hit #1 on Artificial Analysis's leaderboard for prompt adherence — Curious Refuge called it "one of the most obedient models we've ever tested." Three things no peer matches: planning-first generation (it reasons about composition before rendering), multimodal output (4 visual directions from one prompt to pick from), and four explicit variants — raster up to 2K plus native editable SVG via the V4 Vector tier.

Weak spotStruggles with transparent materials (wine glass tests fail), specific fictional characters (game/show IPs come out generic), and complex multi-object compositions. Pick over Recraft V4 whenYou need raster and SVG from one model, or want side-by-side multimodal variants from a single prompt.
Ideogram V3 Character

One reference photo → consistent character across pose, outfit, lighting, and scene — no LoRA training, no 10–30-image dataset. In Rival's 16-challenge head-to-head it ties dead-even with Nano Banana 2; reviewers split it as Ideogram wins on identity (same face), Nano Banana wins on scene logic (same lighting and proportions).

Weak spotDrift over long sessions; lower max resolution than base V3. Pick over a Flux LoRA whenYou have one good reference photo and don't want to assemble a dataset and train a model.
NVIDIA Consistory

An NVIDIA Research approach that delivers character consistency without any training or fine-tuning — it shares internal model activations across a generation series instead of injecting a learned identity. Compatible with ControlNet; first method to extend training-free consistency to multi-subject scenes. Worth flagging: NVIDIA's hosted demo now warns users to "transition to another model to avoid service interruption" — read as soft sunset.

Weak spotBackgrounds can bleed between images when masks are imperfect; style misalignment when shared hidden states encode both semantic and visual features; hosted service flagged for wind-down. Pick over Ideogram V3 Character whenYou specifically need multi-subject training-free consistency — but verify the hosted endpoint is still live before building on it.
Recraft V4 brand systems ★

The model designers use when "shipped, on-brand" matters more than "wow, what an image." Brand Style Lock takes 5–10 reference images and stamps that visual language across hundreds of outputs — Chatfuel's senior designer used it to ship 80+ on-brand 3D Play-Doh-style assets in weeks. Generates real editable SVGs (clean Bezier paths, opens in Figma/Illustrator), not raster-pretending-to-be-vector. Topped HuggingFace's text-to-image leaderboard at V3 and again at V4.

Weak spotEditorial bias — outputs lean "fashion campaign," which feels constraining when you want stock-neutral or weird-and-creative. Not for raw photorealism. Pick over Flux 2 Pro whenThe deliverable is brand assets, logos, illustrations, or icons — not a photograph.
Seedream V4

ByteDance's poster-and-brand-layout model. Designer-level composition with cinematic color grading and filmic grain — handles small text down to 8–12pt, Chinese characters, and complex multi-line layouts with "vector-grade kerning" per fal.ai's V4.5 review. Purpose-built for what Canva users actually want: clean hierarchy, readable typography, polished commercial finish, 4K native output.

Weak spotAesthetic skews plasticky and commercial — not the pick for experimental or fine-art work; complex curved text still trips it up. Pick over Ideogram V3 whenYou need legible typography plus full cinematic art direction (color grading, lighting, composition) — not just clean text on a flat layout.
Recraft V3

Recraft's pre-rebuild flagship — still the broader-purpose recommendation in Recraft's own docs. Two things V3 does that V4 doesn't crisply replace: precise positional text placement (V4 sharpened text quality but dropped that control), and a less opinionated "design taste" — easier when you want stock-neutral output instead of V4's editorial flair.

Weak spotLower default resolution (1024 vs V4 Pro's 2048); behind V4 on lighting realism and material rendering. Pick over Recraft V4 whenYou need text placed at an exact location, or V4's editorial bias is wrong for the brief.
Stable Diffusion 3.5

Still the answer when "open weights" is a hard requirement — self-hosting, on-prem deploys, custom fine-tunes you fully control. Community license is free up to $1M ARR. The honest 2026 read from comparison guides: "awkward middle ground — more VRAM than SDXL, less quality than Flux, minimal community." Survives mostly on its dreamlike artistic flair and the licensing story, not raw quality.

Weak spot18GB VRAM with no widely-used quantization; LoRA/ControlNet ecosystem is materially thinner than Flux's. Pick over Flux Dev whenYou need a self-hostable open-weight base with a commercial-friendly community license.
Luma Photon

Photoreal generation at fast-tier prices — Photon at ~$0.015/1080p image, Flash variant at $0.004, 4–7× cheaper than Flux at scale. 4MP native. Built on the same architecture as Luma's Ray video models, so outputs feed cleanly into Luma's video pipeline as starting frames. Single-reference character consistency lets you lock a face across a storytelling sequence without LoRA training.

Weak spotText rendering is notably weak — letters garble even on simple prompts; character drift accumulates over long sequences. Pick over Flux 2 Pro whenGenerating at very high volume on a budget, or the image is the starting frame for a Luma video.
Minimax Image 01

A specialist hiding behind a generalist label. Dominates ImageBattle's Architecture & Interiors category — handles reflections, glass, structural beams, and sub-surface skin scattering exceptionally well. Outputs have a built-in gloss / 3D-render aesthetic that flatters product mockups and modern interior viz.

Weak spotForces that high-gloss 3D look on everything — actively bad at flat 2D, anime, or Ghibli-style work; English-language docs are thin. Pick over Flux 2 Pro whenRendering interiors, glossy product hero shots, or anything that benefits from the "3D toy" aesthetic.
Bria IP-clean

The legally-safe choice — trained exclusively on licensed data from Getty, Shutterstock, Alamy, and Envato, with contractual IP indemnification on every output. SOC 2, ISO 27001, GDPR, and EU AI Act compliant; deploys on-prem or fully air-gapped. Used by enterprises where "where did this training data come from?" is a procurement question, not a curiosity.

Weak spotAesthetic ceiling is lower than web-scraped models — the licensed dataset is narrower than the open web, and reviewers acknowledge the gap. Pick over Flux / Imagen / ChatGPT whenLegal indemnification is the binding constraint, not image quality.

Image Editing & Compositing

Edit

These models take an existing image and modify it — changing elements, swapping backgrounds, inpainting regions, or applying text-guided transformations. The quality bar here is different: it's about consistency and faithfulness to the original, not raw generation quality.

Flux Kontext editing ★

The most-discussed image editing model of 2025. Pixel-level local editing — change a car's color, swap text, replace objects — while maintaining everything else. ~3 second generation, 6x faster than diffusion-based alternatives. Strong context preservation.

Choose whenTargeted local edits, product photo variations, text replacement in images. Your go-to editing model.
Flux Kontext Multi Image

Extends Flux Kontext to accept multiple reference images. Enables style transfer from one image to a scene, or placing consistent subjects into new environments.

Choose whenYou have multiple reference images to blend or need to keep a character/product consistent.
Flux Kontext LoRA

Flux Kontext with LoRA fine-tune support. Lets you customize the editing model for specific subjects, styles, or brand assets by training on a small image set.

Choose whenRepetitive editing workflows with consistent brand/product; personalized model behavior.
Nano Banana / Nano Banana 2 / Nano Banana Pro

Google Gemini 2.5 Flash image capability — text-guided editing with natural language understanding. Very flexible and "conversational" — you can chain edits like text instructions. Nano Banana Pro adds quality; Nano Banana 2 adds speed improvements.

Choose whenComplex multi-step edits described in natural language; creative flexibility over pixel-precise control.
ChatGPT Images 2.0 Edit new

OpenAI's image editing model. Instruction-following editing using the GPT Image architecture. Strong for coherent edits that make "logical" sense in context.

Choose whenYou want reliable, instruction-driven edits with a polished output aesthetic.
Seedream V4.5 Edit / V5 Edit

ByteDance's editing models (Seedream family). Excellent for instruction following and content preservation — faithful to the original image. V5 improves on instruction adherence with reward-based optimization.

Choose whenYou need faithful edits that preserve the original image closely while applying targeted changes.
Qwen Image Edit Plus / 2511

Alibaba's Qwen editing model. Best for precise text edits within images and supports chained multi-step operations (e.g. "change the background, then update the text"). Top performer for Chinese text editing.

Choose whenTargeted text replacement, chained operations, multilingual image editing.
Qwen Edit Multiangle

A Qwen variant supporting multi-angle or multi-reference editing scenarios. Useful for editing product imagery across different views consistently.

Choose whenProduct photography across angles; multi-view consistency edits.
Runway Gen-4 Image

Runway's image generation/editing model, closely tied to their video pipeline. Useful for generating frames consistent with cinematic style, or creating starting images for video generation workflows.

Choose whenYour workflow feeds into video generation; cinematic and motion-ready imagery.
Wan 2.7

An open-source model (originally a video model) with image editing capability. Known for consistent character rendering and strong motion-aware editing.

Choose whenCharacter-driven edits, open-source workflow requirements.
Flux Fill Pro

Inpainting specialist from the Flux family. Fill or replace masked regions while preserving surrounding context. More controlled than general Flux Kontext.

Choose whenRemoving objects, filling in gaps, replacing specific masked regions cleanly.
Flux 2 Inpaint [Klein 9B]

Flux 2-based inpainting using the Klein 9B model. Strong quality for masked region fills within Flux 2's photorealism range.

Choose whenHigh-quality inpainting within a photorealistic image; pairs well with Flux 2 source images.
Flux Dev LoRA / Flux Dev LoRA Inpaint

Open Flux Dev with LoRA fine-tuning, plus an inpainting variant. Powerful for custom-trained editing workflows. The inpaint version allows masked region fill using a LoRA-customized model.

Choose whenYou need a customized inpainting model trained on specific subjects or brand assets.
Flux Pro Outpaint / SD3 Outpaint

Extend images beyond their original canvas — useful for adding background, making portrait images landscape, or expanding compositions. Flux Pro Outpaint is higher quality; SD3 version is more flexible/experimental.

Choose whenExtending a crop, adapting an image to a different aspect ratio, adding more scene.
SD3 Inpaint

Stable Diffusion 3's inpainting model. Solid quality with the flexibility of the open-weights SD3 ecosystem.

Choose whenSD3 is your base model of choice and you need masked fill.
Bria Inpaint / Bria Content-Aware Fill

Bria's inpainting and fill tools. Same licensing advantage as Bria's generator — commercially clean, safe for enterprise use. Content-aware fill intelligently fills based on surrounding pixels.

Choose whenClient work with IP concerns; enterprise projects needing legally clean provenance.
SD3 Remove Background / SD3 Content-Aware Fill

Stable Diffusion 3 utility tools. Background removal extracts subjects; content-aware fill intelligently replaces removed areas using scene context.

Choose whenQuick background removal and fill within an SD3-based workflow.
Replace Background / Bria Replace Background

Dedicated background replacement. Extracts subject, replaces background based on prompt. Bria version has the clean licensing. Generic version uses a community model.

Choose whenProduct photography swaps, portrait background changes, e-commerce imagery.
Relight 2.0

Re-lights an existing image with new lighting conditions. Change warm to cool light, add dramatic shadows, simulate golden hour, etc. Niche but irreplaceable for product/portrait work.

Choose whenProduct photography with inconsistent lighting; adapting an image to a new environment's lighting mood.
Image To Image

Classic img2img — use an existing image as a "seed" that the model transforms according to a new prompt, with adjustable strength. The foundational image editing workflow from early diffusion models.

Choose whenStyle transfer, rough-to-refined transformations, using a sketch as a starting point.
Sketch to Image

Converts rough sketches or line art into rendered images. Preserves the composition and structure of your sketch while applying realistic or stylized rendering.

Choose whenDesign ideation — go from napkin sketch to visual in seconds.
Flux ControlNet & LoRA

Flux with ControlNet for structural guidance (edges, depth, pose) plus LoRA customization. Gives you precise compositional control over generated images.

Choose whenYou need exact control over pose, composition, or structural elements in the output.
Stable Diffusion ControlNets

SD's ControlNet suite — the original implementation. Wide ecosystem of edge/pose/depth/normal map guidance. More mature tooling and community resources than Flux ControlNet.

Choose whenYou need precise structural guidance and prefer the mature SD ControlNet ecosystem.
Kolors Virtual Try On

Specialist model for garment/product try-on — overlays clothing or accessories onto a person or mannequin image. Built for fashion/e-commerce.

Choose whenFashion e-commerce, apparel visualization, clothing design presentation.

Vector & SVG

Vector

This is where Recraft dominates almost completely. True SVG output (not just a raster image pretending to be a vector) is rare — only a handful of models actually do it.

Recraft V3 SVG vector ★

Generates actual SVG vector files from prompts. #1 on HuggingFace for logo/icon generation. Clean paths, scalable output. The only model most designers trust for real vector work.

Choose whenLogos, icons, illustrations that need to scale. Any deliverable that goes into Figma/Illustrator as a vector.
Recraft Vectorizer

Converts raster images (PNG/JPG) to SVG vector. Takes any image and traces/converts it. Useful for taking a raster AI output and making it infinitely scalable.

Choose whenYou have a raster image that needs to become a vector — logo cleanup, icon sets, illustration for print.
Recraft Crisp Upscale

Recraft's upscaling model tuned for crisp, clean graphic outputs rather than photographic detail. Better for flat illustration and design work than photography-optimized upscalers.

Choose whenUpscaling illustrations, flat design, icons, or any non-photographic imagery.
Text To Vector

Another text-to-SVG tool focused on typographic and decorative vector generation. Useful for ornamental design, monograms, and text-based vector artwork.

Choose whenType-based vector art, decorative lettering, ornamental design elements.

Upscaling & Enhancement

Upscale

Takes low-res images and makes them larger — but the good ones don't just resize, they add real detail (hallucinate plausible texture). Huge for print production, finishing AI outputs for client delivery, and rescuing old photos.

Topaz Image Upscale photo ★

Topaz Gigapixel — the professional photography standard for upscaling. Deep learning that genuinely adds detail. Particularly strong for photographic images, faces, and fine textures. Desktop-grade quality in a cloud tool.

Choose whenPhotography, portraits, realistic imagery where you need maximum photographic fidelity.
Topaz Sharpen

Topaz's dedicated sharpening model. Recovers detail from soft or motion-blurred images. Different from upscaling — it enhances clarity at the current resolution.

Choose whenBlurry source images, adding punch to soft AI outputs before delivery.
Magnific Upscale AI art ★

Magnific is the gold standard for upscaling AI-generated images specifically. Understands and preserves the aesthetic of AI art. Supports up to 16x with adjustable HDR, Resemblance, and Creativity sliders. Also bundles the Mystic generator.

Choose whenFinishing AI-generated images for high-resolution delivery. Illustrations, concept art, AI portraits.
Magnific Skin Enhancer

A Magnific mode focused specifically on realistic skin — adds pore detail, texture, and lifelike quality to portraits. Essential for beauty/fashion retouching of AI images.

Choose whenPortraits and people imagery where skin needs to look genuinely photographic.
Magnific Precision… (×2)

Magnific's precision upscaling modes — likely different preset configurations (e.g. Precision Photo vs Precision Illustration) optimizing for different source material types.

Choose whenYou know your output type (photo vs illustration) and want optimized upscaling for that category.
Enhancor Image Upscale / Realistic Skin

Enhancor is another upscaling tool with a dedicated skin enhancement mode. Similar positioning to Magnific's skin tools, worth comparing outputs side-by-side for specific subjects.

Choose whenTesting as an alternative to Magnific for portrait enhancement; different aesthetic preferences.

3D Generation

3D

Generate 3D models from images or text. This is the newest and most rapidly evolving category — outputs range from quick mesh references to production-quality 3D assets.

Meshy V6 3D ★

One of the leading text-to-3D and image-to-3D models. Generates textured meshes suitable for game assets, product renders, and design references. Strong on organic shapes and characters.

Choose whenGame/product 3D assets, quick mesh generation from concept imagery.
Rodin V2 / Rodin

Deemos' Rodin — well-regarded for high-fidelity character and object 3D generation. V2 improves detail and topology. Often used for character design, figurines, and product visualization.

Choose whenHigh-quality character 3D models, detailed product visualization, figurine design.
Hunyuan 3D V2.1

Tencent's 3D generation model. Strong quality for architectural and product shapes. Competitive with Meshy and Rodin for clean geometric objects.

Choose whenArchitecture, industrial design, geometric product forms.
Trellis 3D V2 / Trellis

Microsoft Research's 3D generation model. Uses a structured latent space for consistent 3D outputs. Strong for multi-view consistent mesh generation from a single image.

Choose whenSingle-image to 3D, consistency across views matters, exploratory 3D workflows.
SAM 3D – Objects

Based on Meta's Segment Anything Model (SAM), adapted for 3D object segmentation and extraction. Identifies and isolates objects in 3D space.

Choose whenExtracting specific objects for 3D use, augmented reality object isolation.

Specialist & LoRA Tools

Specialist

These are tools with very specific functions, or customization layers (LoRA) that let you personalize a base model with a small set of training images.

Flux 2 Dev LoRA / Flux Dev LoRA

LoRA fine-tuning on Flux Dev. Train the model on 10–30 images of a subject, product, or style, and it generates that subject reliably. The primary customization path for Flux.

Choose whenBrand characters, specific products, consistent people, custom illustration styles.
Flux 2 Pro LoRA

LoRA on the Pro-quality Flux 2 base. Higher-quality outputs than Dev LoRA at the cost of more compute. Use when you need custom subjects AND the best generation quality.

Choose whenHigh-quality client work with brand or character consistency requirements.

Quick Reference: What to Use When

Best all-round image quality Flux 2 Pro or Recraft V4 — neck and neck at the top of leaderboards
Text in images (logos, banners) Ideogram V3 — ~90% text accuracy, unmatched for typographic imagery
Vector / SVG output Recraft V3 SVG — the only model that outputs real scalable vector files
Photorealistic people / portraits Mystic or Imagen 4 — Mystic for skin quality, Imagen 4 for overall polish
Brand assets / style control Recraft V4 — built-in style palette controls are unique to Recraft
Fast, cheap concept exploration Flux Fast or Imagen 3 Fast — iterate freely before committing
Targeted local image edits Flux Kontext — pixel-precise, fast, best context preservation
Natural language editing (chained) Nano Banana Pro — conversational, creative, multi-step edits
Upscaling photography Topaz Image Upscale — the professional photography standard
Upscaling AI-generated art Magnific Upscale — purpose-built for AI art aesthetics, up to 16x
3D model generation Meshy V6 for characters/organics, Rodin V2 for high-fidelity figures
Custom subject / brand consistency Flux 2 Dev LoRA for speed, Flux 2 Pro LoRA for quality
Legally clean (commercial IP) Bria — licensed training data, safest for enterprise client deliverables
Fashion / virtual try-on Kolors Virtual Try On — specialist model for garment visualization
From sketch to render Sketch to Image or Flux ControlNet & LoRA for compositional control
Extending a crop / outpainting Flux Pro Outpaint — best quality; SD3 Outpaint as fallback
